# üõ†Ô∏è ZK-MT21 Amplifier Modification: Bluetooth Disabling and AUX Input Lock  

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Problem  

The **ZK-MT21** amplifier supports Bluetooth connectivity by default, which was unacceptable in one of our projects‚Äîwe require **wired AUX input only**, with no possibility of external connection. *By default, Bluetooth takes priority, and someone could **replace** your beautiful music with <span style="color: red">their rap</span>.*  

After analysis and reverse engineering, we found that:  
- The control chip is similar to **TD5161A**.  
- When Bluetooth connects, the amplifier **automatically disables AUX**.  
- The solution is to **physically bypass the switching logic** and force the device to operate exclusively in AUX mode.  

---  

## ‚úÖ Required Steps  

To fully disable Bluetooth and lock operation to AUX:  

1. **Direct connection of AUX and amplifier signal paths**  
   ‚Üí Connect analog signal chain inputs directly to amplifier inputs (AUX IN ‚Üî AMP IN), bypassing the controller.  

2. **Apply a permanent Enable signal**  
   ‚Üí Connect the amplifier‚Äôs `ENABLE` pin to **GND**‚Äîthis ‚Äúforces‚Äù the amplifier to remain active and ignore Bluetooth signals.  

> üí° **Result:** The amplifier no longer switches to Bluetooth. It works **only** via the physical AUX cable.
